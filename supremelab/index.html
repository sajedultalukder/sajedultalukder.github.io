Our Research at SUPREME Lab

We investigate different fundamental security, privacy and computational challenges empowered by various deep neural network-based intelligent models and privacy-enhancing technologies such as secure multi-party computation (MPC), on-device learning and differential privacy. We particularly target distributed systems comprising of IoT and edge devices to address several practical issues such as communication overhead, resource consumption, cyberattacks and privacy violation. We explore variants of Federated Learning, a framework for collaborative training of (deep) machine learning models under privacy constraints. We are also interested in exploring machine learning applications within computer vision including but not limited to imaging devices, UAV, UGV, USV and drones to develop privacy-enhanced secure solutions.

Another focus of our lab's research is to increase security and privacy in online and geosocial networks. Our primary focus in recent years has been on the automated abuse detection and defense system for online social networks, as well as the development of spammer identification and training tools. We develop novel systems and machine learning methods for detecting fraud and abuse in online and geosocial networks such as Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn, WeChat, and others. The overarching goal of our research is to build and develop machine learning-based automated defensive strategies against fraud and abuse in order to provide a safe and trustworthy online social networking experience.






Featured Projects

AbuSniff: Detection and defenses against abusive Facebook friends

Designed and developed an automated abuse detection system for online social networks like Facebook by detecting whether the Facebook friend is a perceived stranger or abuser.

Demonstrated various security & privacy vulnerabilities that may originate from friend abuse in Facebook.

Performed analysis over crowdsourced user data and implemented AbuSniff, a social network assistant able to predict abusive Facebook friend using supervised learning and take personalized defensive action.

Evaluated the system by conducting user studies on real Facebook users from 25 countries and 6 continents.

(Android, Java, Python, R, Weka)
